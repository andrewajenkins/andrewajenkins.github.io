The Challenge

The team was spending over 40 hours per week—the equivalent of one full-time engineer—manually sifting through test logs to identify and track flaky tests. This was an unsustainable drain on resources and morale, preventing engineers from focusing on high-value work.

The Problem Wasn't Just Time-Consuming—It Was Demoralizing:

  • Manual review of Jenkins console output for 500+ tests across 14 branches
  • Manually categorizing failures (flaky vs. real bugs vs. infrastructure issues)
  • Tracking trends over time in spreadsheets
  • Communicating findings in weekly meetings

Team members, including a junior engineer who would later confirm the 40-hour impact, were buried in this manual work instead of building features or improving test coverage.

The Approach

1. Proactive Initiative

Took the initiative to design and build a comprehensive analytics platform from scratch during personal time—without being asked. Identified the systemic inefficiency and decided to solve it platform-wide.

  • • Recognized pattern: manual toil that could be automated
  • • Proposed solution before building to validate approach
  • • Built during personal time to prove value
  • • Designed for sustainability and team adoption

2. Full-Stack Platform Design

Architected comprehensive system automatically ingesting test results, identifying flaky tests based on historical data, and presenting insights through interactive web dashboard.

Data Pipeline

  • • Jenkins integration
  • • Automated result ingestion
  • • Real-time processing
  • • Historical tracking

Analytics Engine

  • • Flaky test detection
  • • Trend analysis
  • • Pass/fail rate calc
  • • Branch comparison

Dashboard

  • • Real-time monitoring
  • • Filterable views
  • • Drill-down capabilities
  • • Export functionality

3. Cultural Change Through Biweekly Meetings

Initially, the team was hesitant to adopt the tool. Established biweekly quality meetings to drive adoption, train the team, and demonstrate value. Drove tool from abandoned to cornerstone of team workflow.

  • • Founded E2E Reliability Meeting (2+ years running)
  • • Hands-on training for team members
  • • Troubleshooting workshops
  • • Data-driven quality discussions

The Solution

Key Platform Features

Intelligent Flake Detection

Analyzes pass/fail patterns across builds to identify intermittent failures. Uses historical data to distinguish between flaky tests and real bugs.

Historical Trend Analysis

Tracks test health over time across all 14 product branches. Enables proactive identification of degrading test quality.

E2E Reliability Meeting Automation

Generates weekly reports automatically, providing data-driven insights for quality discussions. Became foundation of 2+ year meeting series.

Cross-Branch Comparison

Compare test stability across all product branches, identifying branch-specific issues and patterns.

The Impact

Quantified Results

  • Manual monitoring: 40+ hours/week → under 3 hours
  • Equivalent to freeing up 1 full-time engineer
  • 2+ years of continuous production use
  • Monitors 500+ tests across 14 branches continuously

Strategic Value

  • Became critical infrastructure for quality organization
  • Foundation for E2E Reliability Meeting (2+ years)
  • Enabled data-driven decision making on test quality
  • Improved team morale by eliminating demoralizing toil
  • Senior management cited as sign of team maturity

Lessons Learned

Internal Platforms Need UX Thought

Developer tools need as much UX consideration as customer products. The success of e2e-tracker proved that well-designed internal platforms drive adoption and deliver exponential value.

Automation's Real Value: Human Focus

Automation isn't about replacing humans—it's about freeing them for higher-level work. The 40 hours saved weren't "lost"—they were redirected to building features and improving quality.

Data Visualization Transforms Raw Data

The same data existed in Jenkins logs, but without visualization and analysis, it was unusable. Turning data into actionable insights is where platform engineering creates value.

Building for Maintainability Matters

Still running after 2+ years with minimal maintenance. Investing in clean architecture, good documentation, and sustainable design pays compound returns.

Transferable Insights

This platform demonstrates skills applicable to any organization:

  • Proactive problem identification: Recognizing systemic inefficiency and proposing platform solutions before being asked
  • Full-stack platform development: From data pipelines to analytics engines to interactive dashboards
  • Driving adoption through cultural change: Building the tool is half the battle—driving adoption requires training, evangelism, and persistence
  • Long-term thinking: Building sustainable platforms that run for 2+ years with minimal maintenance

Lasting Impact

E2E Tracker demonstrated the power of building internal platforms to solve team-wide problems. The success of this project informed the approach to later AI platform work—understanding that developer tools need to be as thoughtfully designed as customer-facing products.

This platform continues to serve as the backbone of test reliability efforts, enabling the team to focus on building new capabilities rather than firefighting flaky tests.
