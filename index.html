AI-Powered Test Automation Platform (TAP)

The Problem

Test creation was a slow, manual process, often taking days. It required deep, specialized domain knowledge of the clinical trial application, creating a bottleneck and making it difficult to scale test automation efforts.

My Solution

I architected and built a production-grade, internal RAG-based platform using Claude Code. This system functions as a conversational AI partner for engineers. It's built on a microservice architecture, including:

  • data-setup-generator: Generates complex test data from natural language.
  • test-generator: Creates Playwright test scripts conversationally.
  • test-integrator: Integrates the new scripts into our existing framework with proper page objects.

The Outcome

TAP reduced test creation time from days to minutes, fundamentally changing our workflow. It empowers engineers to generate complete, framework-compliant tests simply by describing their requirements in plain English, directly accelerating our ability to deliver quality.

Clinical Trials Context Engine

The Problem

For the AI (TAP) to be useful, it couldn't just write generic code. It needed to understand the deep, complex, and highly specific domain of clinical trial data models (RTSM). A generic LLM has no concept of this specialized business logic.

My Solution

I built the "brain" for TAP. This involved creating a specialized RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) knowledge base from the ground up. I wrote scripts to ingest and process our application's entire set of Django models and existing test patterns. This gave the AI the specific "context" it needed to understand our business logic.

The Outcome

This Context Engine is what makes TAP truly intelligent. It enables the AI to generate code that is context-aware and specific to our clinical trials domain. It directly assists engineers by accelerating the difficult translation of complex business requirements into accurate test code.

Team Efficiency Infrastructure (E2E Tracker)

The Problem

Our team was spending over 40 hours per week—the equivalent of one full-time engineer—manually sifting through test logs to identify and track flaky tests. This was an unsustainable drain on resources and morale.

My Solution

I took the initiative to design and build a full-stack analytics platform from scratch. The system automatically ingests test results, identifies flaky tests based on historical data, and presents them in an interactive web dashboard. The tech stack includes:

  • Angular: For the interactive frontend dashboard.
  • Django: For the backend API.
  • MongoDB: As the database to store and query test results.

The Outcome

The "E2E Tracker" reduced manual monitoring from 40+ hours/week to under 3. It became critical infrastructure for our team's quality initiatives, formed the basis of our E2E Reliability meeting, and freed up an entire engineer's worth of time to focus on building new automation rather than chasing flakes.
